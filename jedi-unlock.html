The Path to the Jedi

Not a rumor, not a forum legend — this is the real, verified questline running on SWGReturns Pre-CU right now: badges, an old man in the woods, a Force crystal, a whole village that lives on its own clock, and a choice between the Light and the Dark. Here's exactly how it works.

Overview

Every Jedi on this server walks the same road, and it is long by design. There is no Holocron to grind and no menu toggle — the galaxy itself has to notice you first. You'll earn the attention of the Force through your deeds, get pulled into a hidden Force-sensitive village on Dathomir, help that village survive four escalating phases of danger, face down a Dark Jedi named Mellichae, complete the trials every Padawan must pass, and finally choose a side in the Force Rank System as a full Jedi Knight.

This guide walks the whole thing in order, stage by stage, exactly as it plays out on the server.

No profession swap needed: you don't need to be any particular class to start this. You just need to go out into the galaxy and rack up the right kinds of achievements — badges are earned passively through normal exploring, questing, and mastering professions.

Step 1 — Earn Your Badges

The Force doesn't send anyone to knock on your door until you've proven yourself across five categories of in-game badges. You need all five — hit every threshold below and the wheels start turning automatically, wherever you happen to be standing at the time:

2
Dangerous Exploration badges
5

1
Master (profession) badge
1
Content badge

You can check your progress at any time with the /checkForceStatus command — it'll tell you how many of the five categories you've completed so far.

No village trip required to start: despite what you might have heard, you do not need to travel anywhere special to trigger this. The moment your last badge lands, you're flagged — the game is now just waiting for the right moment to send someone to find you.

Step 2 — The Old Man & the Crystal

Somewhere between five and ten minutes after your badges complete, an old man will appear near you out in the field — not in a cantina, not at a waypoint, just wherever you're standing. Approach him and talk.

1

Take the crystal

In the conversation, choose the option to accept the Force crystal. If you instead pick "perhaps another time," he'll simply walk off — and he will come back, but the wait grows longer every time you stall (an extra day after two brush-offs, and more after that). Don't waste his patience.

2

Survive the ambush

Five to ten minutes after you're holding the crystal, two Sith Shadow agents will jump you. Fight them off.

3

Loot & read the first datapad

Looting the first Sith Shadow you kill drops a waypoint datapad. Using it points you toward a hidden camp — and pulls in two more Sith Shadows to fight when you arrive.

4

Loot & read the second datapad

Clear the camp, loot the first Sith Shadow there for a second datapad, and use it. This is the one that finally gives you a waypoint to Dathomir.

5

Walk into the village

Follow the waypoint onto Dathomir and simply walk into the marked area. The moment you do, you're awarded the Force-Sensitive Novice title and officially become Force Sensitive.

Do not die during the ambush. If the Sith Shadows kill you before you loot that first datapad, they take the crystal back — you lose it entirely and get sent all the way back to waiting for the old man to visit again. Come prepared, or bring a friend.

Step 3 — The Force-Sensitive Village

Becoming Force Sensitive doesn't unlock Jedi by itself — it unlocks access to the Village, a community-wide storyline in four phases. This is where the guide most people give you gets it wrong: the phases aren't something you race through solo on your own schedule.

The village runs on one shared clock for the whole server. Each phase lasts 24 hours before it automatically rotates to the next (1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → back to 1). If you don't finish a phase's task before the clock turns over, it resets — you'll simply pick it back up next time that phase comes around, up to a few days later. Plan your sessions with the daily rollover in mind.

Phase 1 — Settling In

Treat sick villagers by diagnosing and administering the right medicine, solve a sensor-calibration crafting puzzle, rescue five captured villagers from Sith Shadow camps, and patrol the village perimeter — engage the small raiding parties, but steer clear of the large ones, they're not meant to be soloed.

Phase 2 — Under Pressure

Run six supply-fetch missions out to Endor and back, work through an eight-stage escalating combat gauntlet against Sith Shadow camps (capped at three stages per day, so pace yourself), and pitch in on a community crafting effort to build the village's defenses — turn in your own components to finish your personal quest, and the single best contributor server-wide earns a bonus prize.

Phase 3 — Counterstrike

A second eight-stage combat gauntlet, plus the big one: hunt down a Sith Shadow base, use a looted remote to drop its shields, fight through five named guards, then capture the camp's commander and escort them out alive. Guard that escort closely — if you go down, the commander breaks free and anyone else nearby can swoop in and finish the capture instead. A larger community crafting push (this time for shield components) runs in parallel.

Phase 4 — Defend the Village

Raiders come for the village itself — pick a combat role and rack up kills, or support the defense by healing your fellow villagers, either counts toward completion. Alongside that, a solo engineering chain sends you after a downed satellite core and into a wire-splicing puzzle — get it wrong three times in a row and the core is ruined, so slow down and think it through.

Nice bonus: most of these sub-quests also permanently unlock a Force-Sensitive skill branch as a reward on top of village progress — a little taste of what's coming once you're a full Jedi.

Step 4 — The Outro: Mellichae

Finish all four phases, and the old man tracks you down one more time, the same way he found you at the very start. Talk to him, and he leads you to a final confrontation.

You'll be sent to a hidden camp guarded by the Dark Jedi Mellichae and her enforcer Daktar Bloodmoon, backed up by Sith Shadow muscle. The camp holds four Force shrines that passively heal the pair as long as they're standing — destroy all four shrines first, or you'll be fighting an unwinnable war of attrition. Bring the shrines down, then bring Mellichae down.

Defeating her closes out the Force-Sensitive Village storyline for good — and opens the door to the Padawan Trials.

Step 5 — Padawan Trials

This is where you stop being coordinated by the village and start being tested one-on-one. Padawan Trials are progressed at Force Shrines scattered across the galaxy — over forty of them, spread across ten planets. Find one, kneel, and choose to meditate to begin or continue.

You'll need to have already picked up at least four Force-Sensitive skill branches from your time in the village to even qualify — one more reason not to skip those side-rewards in Step 3.

The Hunt

Track down and kill 20 sludge panthers, reporting to the ecologist who set you on the trail.

The Soldier's Errand

An old soldier sends you after a specific outlaw leader. Choose your words carefully in conversation — the wrong response here can fail the trial outright, not just the fight.

The Lightsaber

Always the final trial. Craft a training lightsaber, then tune a color crystal into it with your own hands. The moment this one clicks into place, you are a Padawan.

Three strikes and you're reset. Fail a trial three times and the whole Padawan Trials progress is wiped — you'll need to start over from the first trial. You can also voluntarily quit and reset at the shrine, or just restart your current trial without penalty if you'd rather re-roll it.

Complete the third trial and you're rewarded on the spot: the Padawan title, a Padawan robe, and a proud little system-wide fanfare announcing your new rank.

Step 6 — Knight Trials: Light or Dark

Knight Trials are different in one big way: you don't have to go find them. Once you've fully mastered at least one Jedi skill box as a Padawan, the trials kick off automatically the next time you log in, and the game assigns you one specific shrine on another planet as your personal checkpoint for the rest of the sequence.

From there, it's a fixed gauntlet of hunts, each one unlocking the next as soon as you hit the kill count — no talking, no travel back to a quest giver, just track and hunt:

  1. 19 Tusken Raiders
  2. 8 Ancient Bull Rancors
  3. 8 Stintaril Prowlers
  4. 3 Blurrg Raptors
  5. 3 Enraged Kimogilas
  6. 2 Peko-Peko Albatrosses
  7. 2 Graul Marauders
  8. Choose your Council — Light or Dark
  9. 15 enemy soldiers (Imperial Storm Commandos for Light, Rebel Commandos for Dark)

Choosing sides has consequences. Your Galactic Civil War faction has to match the Council you pick — Imperials can't join the Light Council, Rebels can't join the Dark, not without dropping their current faction status first. Think about which side you actually want to be on before you get to stage 8.

Finish the final hunt and you become a full Jedi Knight — Light or Dark — complete with a matching robe, a waypoint to your side's enclave on Yavin 4, your faction locked in and made Overt, and entry into the server's Force Rank System for further endgame progression as a Jedi.

Tips & Warnings

  • Check your badges anytime with /checkForceStatus so you know exactly what's left before the old man can find you.
  • Don't stall the old man. Declining him repeatedly makes him take longer and longer to come back.
  • Gear up before the Sith Shadow ambush. Dying costs you the crystal and resets your intro progress.
  • Watch the village's daily phase clock. Unfinished phase quests reset at rollover — try to start tasks early in a phase, not late.
  • Guard the Counterstrike commander with your life. Other players can steal your capture if you fall while escorting.
  • Clear all four shrines before fighting Mellichae or the healing loop will outlast you.
  • Play your words carefully in the Padawan "Soldier's Errand" trial — a bad dialogue choice can fail it on the spot.
  • Decide Light or Dark before Knight Trials stage 8 and make sure your GCW faction lines up with your choice.
